Subject: POSH Policy - Prevention of Sexual Harassment
To All Employees,

{{company_name}} is committed to providing a safe, secure, and respectful working environment for all employees, free from any form of sexual harassment. This policy is formulated in accordance with the provisions of the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013, and applies to all employees regardless of gender.

1. Definition of Sexual Harassment:

Sexual harassment includes any unwelcome sexually determined behavior, whether directly or by implication, including but not limited to: physical contact and advances; a demand or request for sexual favors; sexually colored remarks; showing pornography; any other unwelcome physical, verbal, or non-verbal conduct of a sexual nature. It also includes implied or explicit promise of preferential treatment, implied or explicit threat of detrimental treatment, implied or explicit threat about present or future employment status, interference with work or creating an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment, and humiliating treatment likely to affect health or safety.

2. Scope:

This policy applies to all employees (permanent, temporary, contractual, trainees, and interns), visitors, clients, and any third party associated with the organization. It covers incidents occurring at the workplace, which includes the office premises, any place visited by the employee arising out of or during the course of employment, and any transportation provided by the employer for the purpose of commuting to and from the workplace.

3. Internal Complaints Committee (ICC):

The company has constituted an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) as required under the Act. The ICC is responsible for receiving and redressing complaints of sexual harassment.

4. Complaint Procedure:

Any aggrieved employee may make a written complaint to the ICC within three months from the date of the incident or the last incident in case of a series of incidents. The complaint should contain details of the incident(s), the name of the respondent, and any supporting evidence or witnesses. The ICC Presiding Officer may extend the time limit by a further period of three months if satisfied that circumstances were such that prevented the complainant from filing within the initial period.

5. Inquiry Process:

Upon receiving a complaint, the ICC shall proceed to make an inquiry in accordance with the principles of natural justice. The ICC may, before initiating an inquiry, at the request of the complainant, take steps to settle the matter through conciliation (provided no monetary settlement is made). If conciliation fails or is not opted for, the ICC shall initiate a formal inquiry. Both parties will be given an opportunity to be heard, present witnesses, and submit evidence. The inquiry shall be completed within 90 days from the date of receipt of the complaint.

6. Timelines:

The ICC shall complete the inquiry within 90 days and submit its report with recommendations to the employer within 10 days of completing the inquiry. The employer shall act upon the recommendations within 60 days of receiving the report.

7. Penalties:

If the allegation is proved, the ICC may recommend disciplinary action against the respondent, which may include: written apology, warning, reprimand, withholding of promotion, withholding of pay rise or increments, termination from service, undergoing counseling sessions, or carrying out community service. The nature of the penalty will be commensurate with the severity of the offense.

8. Confidentiality Provisions:

The identity and addresses of the complainant, respondent, witnesses, any information relating to conciliation and inquiry proceedings, recommendations of the ICC, and the action taken by the employer shall not be published, communicated, or made known to the public, press, or media in any manner. Breach of confidentiality shall be treated as misconduct and may result in disciplinary action.

All employees are encouraged to report any incidents of sexual harassment and are assured that no retaliation or adverse action will be taken against anyone who makes a complaint in good faith.
