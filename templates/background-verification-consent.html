As part of the onboarding process at {{company_name}}, all new employees are required to undergo a comprehensive background verification. This process is conducted to ensure the integrity and safety of our workplace and to comply with applicable regulatory requirements.

By signing this consent form, you hereby authorize {{company_name}} and its designated third-party verification agencies to conduct the following checks:

1. Educational Qualifications. Verification of all academic degrees, diplomas, certifications, and professional qualifications as declared by you in your application and supporting documents. This includes contacting educational institutions and certification bodies to confirm the authenticity and validity of the credentials provided.

2. Previous Employment. Verification of your employment history, including dates of employment, job titles, roles and responsibilities, reason for leaving, and eligibility for rehire with all previous employers as listed in your resume or application form.

3. Criminal Record Check. A search of applicable criminal databases and court records to ascertain whether there are any criminal convictions, pending cases, or other legal proceedings against you. This may include checks at the local, state, and national levels as deemed appropriate.

4. Identity and Address Verification. Verification of your identity through government-issued identification documents (such as Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, or Voter ID) and confirmation of your current and permanent residential addresses.

5. Reference Checks. The Company may contact professional and personal references provided by you to gather information regarding your character, work ethic, and professional conduct.

You understand and agree that: (a) any misrepresentation or falsification of information may result in the withdrawal of the employment offer or termination of employment; (b) the results of the background verification shall remain confidential and will be used solely for employment-related decisions; (c) you have the right to request a copy of the verification report and to dispute any inaccurate findings.
